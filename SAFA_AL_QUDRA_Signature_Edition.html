PROJECT EXECUTION PROCESS

01
Quotation and Value Engineering
Understanding the project and its component is the major contributor to success, therefore at SAFA AL QUDRA projects our team of engineers convenes to analyze the specifications, outline the products, avail resources. Enhance performance and system function in order to quote the best value for the best product that match and serve specification.
02
Order Approval and Planning
Once order approved the team at SAFA AL QUDRA immediately start planning and scheduling for the project along the line schedule of the General Contracts using MS Projects software tools. This process starts by submitting the project schedule, to GC and obtain approval of Consultant and GCC.
03
Submittals and Approvals
It is very important to identify the system to be used and its components to consultant / architect and obtain approvals prior to procurement, so this process involve submittal of system samples, accessories, hardware, and glass that meet or exceed the project specifications, supported by manufacturer catalogue and products description or performance.
04
Shop Drawings and Material Procurement
Shop is our art of design at SAFA AL QUDRA project, our qualified engineers are so articulate about drawing the best design that produce state of art building complying with the architect outlines and elevations. Submittals of shop drawings come in phases that concurrent in line of project schedule, and the same is true for consultant approvals. On a parallel track our procurement of material starts and our purchasing team work professionally to secure all materials at the time ready for fabrication to ensure complete delivery and proper installation.

HEALTH, SAFETY & ENVIRONMENT

Introduction / Foreword

This manual is a guide for establishing and maintaining safe working conditions, and for promoting safe work practices by management and employees. We hope that implementing these safety guidelines will set a good example for the entire all.

We recognize its responsibility to provide a safe and healthful working environment. This shall include making reasonable efforts to promptly investigate and address health and safety issues, not requiring employees to perform tasks that are dangerous to their health and safety without adequate training and safety equipment as determined by applicable state and federal laws, and making information on hazardous materials readily accessible.

All of us must practice safe work habits at all times. Only a diligent effort on everyone's part will prevent injuries and illnesses.

HSE Mission

The mission of SAFA AL QUDRA is to work towards the elimination of work place injuries and illnesses. We are determined to:
1. Increase attention to training and the capability of the supervisor and worker to identify and deal with work place health and safety hazards.
2. Increase the understanding and awareness by all employees of safety and health hazards associated with their jobs.
3. Maintain and update the joint Safety Manual.
4. Promptly investigate and respond to service maintenance unsafe working conditions forms.
5. Study job safety and health analyses of tasks causing the most serious and frequent injuries and illnesses.

Unsafe Working Conditions

1. Whenever a dangerous working condition exists that requires immediate corrective action, the employee(s) should notify their supervisor at once.
2. The Management shall investigate the alleged dangerous condition and render a decision within two (2) working days.
3. Where a working condition is thought to be unsafe but does not require immediate corrective action, the employee(s) should fill out Service Maintenance form.
4. An employee or group of employees who refuse a work assignment because of a reasonable belief that to perform such work would endanger physical safety should use the expedited complaint procedure.

Supervisor's Responsibility

1. Provide safety instruction, training and encourage safe work performance.
2. Ensure the repair or replacement of all defective equipment.
3. Inspect all equipment and tell each employee the proper PPE that must be used.
4. Encourage employees safety suggestions.
5. Counsel and discipline employees who fail to use required safety equipment.
6. Investigate all accidents and complete detailed Accident Report Forms.
7. Be aware of all occupational health hazards or toxic substances.
8. Request assistance from EH&S where an occupational hazard may exist.
9. Ensure new employees receive required physicals and tests.
10. Prohibit use of alcoholic beverages and narcotics while on duty.

Employee's Responsibility

1. Use maximum care and good judgment in preventing accidents and injuries.
2. Report unsafe conditions, equipment, practices, and on the job injuries.
3. Abide by all safety rules and regulations.
4. Understand your supervisor's instructions. If you do not know how to do the job safely, ask questions.
5. Do not engage in tasks which you believe might be unsafe without consulting your supervisor.
6. Observe all posted warning signs.
7. Clean up the work area and equipment upon job completion.
8. Before you start work on any electrical system, make a personal inspection.
9. Know the substances you work with which are hazardous. Review MSDS.
10. The use of alcoholic beverages or narcotics while on duty is prohibited.

Eye Protection

Eye protection is required for certain jobs and areas of work.
1. All employees exposed to flying objects, particles, molten metal, gases, fumes will be provided with appropriate eye protection.
2. An eyewash and deluge shower is required where chemical splashes are possible.
3. Foreign objects should be removed from an employee's eye only by a qualified person.
4. Eye protection equipment is available through your department.

Fire Prevention and Safety

If a fire or explosion occurs, activate the building evacuation alarm and call the Fire and Emergency number.
1. Do not use the elevators. Keep calm; shut off gas and equipment before leaving.
2. Stay between your escape route and the fire.
3. If your clothing catches fire, stop, drop and roll.
4. Everyone should be familiar with the location of fire extinguishers and fire hoses.
5. Flammable mixtures must be stored only by approved methods.
6. The campus is a smoke-free environment.
7. Emergency exits should not be locked or blocked.

Protective Clothing & Equipment

It is the employee's responsibility to obtain, use, maintain and clean protective equipment.
1. Avoid loose fitting clothes. Shirts with sleeves and long pants must be worn.
2. Head protection (hard hats) must be worn where there is danger of falling objects.
3. Respirators must be used when adequate ventilation cannot be provided. Employee must be fit-tested.
4. When entering confined spaces, always bring breathing equipment.

Hearing Protection

If the noise level in your work area is high, ask your supervisor to request a sound level survey.
1. All noise hazard areas should be marked.
2. Employees in noise-hazard areas will be provided with hearing protectors and annual training.
3. Hearing protection is required when ambient noise is above 85 decibels.

Equipment Care and Use

Supervisors and employees share the responsibility that equipment and tools are safe.
1. All tools and equipment are to be kept in a clean and repaired condition.
2. Only trained employees should operate machinery.
3. Never exceed the manufacturer's recommended RPM on rotary equipment.
4. All rotary machines must be protected by a proper guard.
5. Electric tools shall be grounded or double-insulated.
6. Use a tool only for the purposes for which it was designed.

Moving and Lifting

1. Do not attempt to carry a load which is more than you can carry safely; get help.
2. Supervisors should see that mechanical equipment is used when manual lifting is unsafe.
3. Wipe off all greasy, slippery objects before handling.
4. Always lift gradually and smoothly, without jerking or twisting.

Ladders

Use only ladders in good repair and equipped with safety shoes.
1. Report any defects to your supervisor.
2. Be sure the ladder is long enough to do the job safely.
3. Fiberglass ladders are preferred when working around electrical equipment.
4. Never use the top three feet of an extension ladder.

Scaffolds

Scaffolds and related ropes and lines should be in good repair.
1. Where a scaffold is over ten feet high, guardrails and toe boards must be installed.
2. Do not install the scaffold in the vicinity of power lines.
3. Each worker must wear an approved safety life belt attached to a lifeline.

Confined Space Entry

A confined space is any enclosed area with limited means of entry which contains known or potential hazards.

Managers shall: Be responsible for all training; authorize only trained employees to enter; ensure health and safety; monitor the worksite.

Employees shall: Observe all rules and precautions; enter only after authorization.

Under no circumstances shall employees: Enter an untested confined space; enter a space known to contain a dangerous atmosphere; enter without authorization.
